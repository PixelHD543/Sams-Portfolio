Digital Manufacturing of Modular Phone Casings

Target: 50,000+ units/year
Achieved: 50,064 units/year
OEE: 87%
Cpk: >1.33

Project Overview – MSc Digital Manufacturing

This 9-week project developed a complete digital manufacturing process for modular phone casings (top and bottom) for "Phones-R-Us". The work spans additive manufacturing, metrology, digital twins, traceability, and process optimisation – a full Industry 4.0 implementation.

Key Outcomes

  • ✅ Process capability improved: Cp 0.8 → 1.47 (Ultimaker S5 → Bambu Lab X1C)
  • ✅ Annual output: 50,064 phones (beating 50,000 target)
  • ✅ OEE: 87% (layout 2 with AMR)
  • ✅ Measurement reliability: GOM ATOS GR&R <5%
  • ✅ Traceability: QR codes + GitHub digital product passport
  • ✅ Energy use: 0.08 kWh per casing

PFMEA – From Failure to Engineered Solution

A full Process Failure Mode Effects Analysis identified three highest-risk failures on the Ultimaker S5:

Highest RPN (Risk Priority Number) failures:

1. Bed adhesion failure     → RPN 280
2. Layer separation         → RPN 210  
3. Dimensional inaccuracy   → RPN 196

Solutions implemented:
→ Switched to Bambu Lab X1C (enclosed, heated chamber)
→ Changed infill from triangular to gyroid (15%)
→ Reduced layer height from 0.2mm to 0.16mm
→ eSUN PLA+ filament (220°C nozzle, 55°C bed)

Result: First successful print with no warping – snap-fits clicked together by hand. A 10-part capability study showed Cp improved from 0.8 to 1.47, with Cpk >1.33 for all critical features.

Digital Twin – Tecnomatix Plant Simulation

Built a full digital twin of the production system, modelling:

  • FESTO CP assembly line: 6 stations with measured cycle times (total 85s per phone)
  • Print farm: 12 Bambu Lab X1C printers, each producing 2 phone sets every 2 hours, running 24/7
  • Kanban buffer: Between printers and assembly line
  • Labour model: 4 workers at 80% efficiency, 8h/day, 5 days/week

Metrology – GOM ATOS Scanning & GR&R

All parts were scanned with the GOM ATOS 3D scanner. A Gauge Repeatability & Reproducibility (GR&R) study confirmed measurement reliability:

  • GR&R result: <5% – excellent measurement system
  • Reference: CAD model from reverse-engineered master top
  • Key features verified: Snap-hook width, slot depth, overall height

The FESTO CP line test was successful – every casing moved smoothly through all six stations, and the finished phone passed the 100-cycle test.

Digital Product Passport – QR + GitHub

Each casing receives a laser-etched QR code linking to a live GitHub page displaying:

  • Printer model and material batch
  • Infill settings and layer height
  • Timestamp of manufacture
  • Inspection results (dimensions vs CAD model)
Example QR payload:
https://github.com/MyRoboticsJourney/phone-casing-traceability/batch-7

Batch-7 page includes:
- Printer: Bambu Lab X1C #03
- Material: eSUN PLA+ White (Batch #2403)
- Settings: 0.16mm layer, 15% gyroid infill
- Dimensions: top width 68.02mm (±0.05), bottom depth 12.48mm (±0.03)
- Status: PASSED inspection

Demonstrated: UR5e collaborative robot reads the QR code and sorts non-conforming parts into a reject bin – fully automated quality control.

Sustainability & Energy Analysis

  • Energy consumption: 0.08 kWh per casing (Bambu Lab X1C)
  • Material: eSUN PLA+ (biodegradable, recyclable)
  • Waste reduction: Additive manufacturing competitive with injection moulding at 50,000 units/year
  • Circular potential: Local PLA recycling reduces transport emissions

Reference: Ashby (2021) – additive manufacturing can be sustainable at medium volumes when waste is recycled locally.
