Digital Manufacturing of Modular Phone Casings

Target: 50,000+ units/year
Achieved: 50,064 units/year
OEE: 87%
Cpk: >1.33

Project Overview – MSc Digital Manufacturing

This 9-week project developed a complete digital manufacturing process for modular phone casings (top and bottom) for "Phones-R-Us". The work spans additive manufacturing, metrology, digital twins, traceability, and process optimisation – a full Industry 4.0 implementation.

Key Outcomes

  • ✅ Process capability improved: Cp 0.8 → 1.47 (Ultimaker S5 → Bambu Lab X1C)
  • ✅ Annual output: 50,064 phones (beating 50,000 target)
  • ✅ OEE: 87% (layout 2 with AMR)
  • ✅ Measurement reliability: GOM ATOS GR&R <5%
  • ✅ Traceability: QR codes + GitHub digital product passport
  • ✅ Energy use: 0.08 kWh per casing

CAD Design & First Prints – Learning from Failure

CAD Model of Phone Casing

The top and bottom casings were modelled in CAD. The bottom casing came from a drawing; the top casing was reverse-engineered from a physical master using GOM ATOS scanning.

Failed Ultimaker S5 Print

Early prints on the open-frame Ultimaker S5 showed severe corner warping, weak layer bonding, and dimensional errors up to ±0.45 mm. PFMEA identified bed adhesion (RPN 280) as the highest risk.

Successful Bambu Lab X1C Print

Switching to the enclosed Bambu Lab X1C with gyroid infill (15%) and eSUN PLA+ eliminated warping. The 10-part capability study showed Cp improved from 0.8 to 1.47.

Metrology – GOM ATOS 3D Scanning & GR&R

GOM ATOS Scan Result

The GOM ATOS scanner produced a detailed point cloud. A Gauge Repeatability & Reproducibility (GR&R) study confirmed <5% variation – an excellent measurement system.

FESTO CP Lab Line

The casings were tested on the actual FESTO CP lab line (6 stations: product dropper, IR depth measurement, assembly table, part press, oven, robotic placement). All passed the 100-cycle test.

Digital Twin – Tecnomatix Plant Simulation

Tecnomatix Simulation Model

The digital twin modelled the full production system: 12 Bambu Lab X1C printers (24/7 operation), FESTO assembly line (85s per phone), Kanban buffer, and 4 workers at 80% efficiency.

Digital Product Passport – QR + GitHub

Laser-Etched QR Code on Casing

Each casing receives a laser-etched QR code on an inside rib. Scanning it opens a GitHub page with printer, material, infill settings, timestamp, and inspection results.

GitHub Traceability Page

The GitHub page shows the complete manufacturing record: printer model, material batch, layer height, infill pattern, and a chart comparing dimensions to the CAD model.
